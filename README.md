## Hi there 👋

# Alan J. Figueroa Ruiz

**Conservation Physiology | Wildlife Medicine and Disease Ecology | Reproductive Biology | One Health**

I am a conservation agronomist and master's student in Biology at the University of Puerto Rico at Mayagüez, working at the intersection of wildlife medicine disease ecology, conservation physiology, environmental health, and community-based science.

My current research focuses on *Toxoplasma gondii* at the land–sea interface in Puerto Rico, with emphasis on environmental transmission pathways, pathogen movement across human-modified landscapes, and conservation implications for the endangered Caribbean manatee.

I am especially interested in how comparative anatomy, reproductive physiology, theriogenology, tissue and cellular biology, molecular diagnostics, bioinformatics, and One Health frameworks can help us understand and protect threatened wildlife populations.

## 🌊 Current projects

- **Manatee Project Puerto Rico**  
  Studying land-to-sea transmission pathways of *Toxoplasma gondii* and their potential implications for Antillean     manatee conservation.

- **Human *Toxoplasma gondii* Epidemiology in Puerto Rico**  
  Analyzing diagnostic records to better understand human exposure patterns and strengthen One Health surveillance     in Puerto Rico.

- **Zebrafish (*Danio rerio*) ZF4 Cells Infection Model**
  Exploring *Toxoplasma gondii* infection in zebrafish (*Danio rerio*) ZF4 cells as a comparative cellular model to    support questions in host–pathogen interactions, tissue biology, and conservation-relevant disease research.

- **WildCiencia**  
  My self as a digital creator developing bilingual science communication focused on wildlife, conservation, disease   ecology, and Puerto Rican science from the field, the laboratory, and the community.

## 🧬 Long-term vision

My long-term goal is to pursue a DVM/PhD pathway and build a research career focused on wildlife medicine, conservation physiology, reproductive biology, disease ecology, and translational One Health science.

I am particularly motivated by research that connects field ecology, veterinary science, molecular biology, computational tools, and community engagement to address conservation challenges affecting threatened species.

## 🌱 Science, community, and conservation

I believe conservation science is strongest when it connects rigorous research with the communities that live closest to the ecosystems we aim to protect. My work is rooted in the idea that communities can build belonging, stewardship, and long-term care for local environments.
